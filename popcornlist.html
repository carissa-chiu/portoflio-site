PopcornList

UX/UI Case Study of a native iOS and Android app.

Project Overview

PopcornList is a mobile app that provides a simple way to track the movies and TV shows you watch. With PopcornList, movie buffs can keep a list of what they want to watch, track movies they've seen, and create custom lists. This app is designed as a native app for iOS and Android devices.


Context & Approach

Through my research I found that many existing similar apps exclude TV Shows and are only available on either iOS or Android, but not both. I saw an opportunity to design an app that provided a simple experience focused mainly on movie list tracking. I took inspiration from similar book and podcast native apps to create a design that would be intuitive, familiar, and user-friendly.

User Flow Diagram

I mapped out the path users would take to accomplish three main actions:

  1. Search for movies and TV shows
  2. Add movies/TV shows to a list
  3. Create a custom list

Wireframes

Using the user flow map, I created low fidelity wireframes for both iOS and Android screens. I followed the design guidelines for iOS and Material Design and selected familiar UI elements that would help the user accomplish tasks easily.

Mood Board & Visual Direction

I took inspiration from cinema visuals, colors, and theatre aesthetics to come up with the the visual design. I wanted to go with a dark theme to match the feeling of a movie theatre.

Style Guide

I used colors associated with theatre popcorn for my main colors of red and yellow, which stood out nicely against the dark background. The bright purple is often associated with a movie theatre which worked well as an accent color. I kept the UI elements simple and functional.
